Plexus Worldwide

As the first UX/UI Designer at Plexus Worldwide, I built the digital design function from scratch — establishing the design system, bridging design and development, and leading three major platform redesigns across e-commerce, an ambassador site builder, and a back-office dashboard serving thousands of users.

Years

2017

Role

UX/UI Designer (Pioneering Role)

Scope

UX/UI Design · Design Systems · Front-End Development · Platform Architecture · Direct Sales UX

Challenge

No design function. No design system. No shared language between marketing and development. A direct sales platform with legally complex compensation rules, Exigo API constraints, thousands of ambassador websites to generate, and a business growing faster than its digital infrastructure.

Solution

Build everything. Start with process, not pixels. Establish the atomic design system first so that every redesign after it could move faster, stay consistent, and bridge the design-to-development gap that was costing the company time and money.

Process / design system architecture

The Sketch component library or the atomic design hierarchy laid out — atoms, molecules, organisms, templates. Show the system architecture.

Three redesigns, each one smarter than the last.

The first redesign set the baseline. The second incorporated A/B test data and ambassador feedback. The third implemented the full design system with Angular component parity. None of them started from scratch — each one was strategic evolution built on what we learned from real users and real data.

Feature 01 image

A side-by-side of redesign generations — show the visible progression. Even two versions is a compelling before/after.

A design system that improved handoff efficiency by 60%.

The atomic design system wasn’t just a Sketch library — it was a shared language. Atoms became Angular components. Design tokens became SCSS variables. The result was a mapping so direct that developers could implement designs without re-interpreting them. That’s the kind of efficiency that compounds across every future project.

Feature 02 image

The component library — show atoms through organisms, or the Sketch-to-Angular mapping side by side.

Industry Challenge

Direct sales UX is uniquely hostile to simplicity. Exigo’s API imposes structural constraints. Compensation plans generate business logic that touches every transaction. State and federal regulations restrict what you can say and how. Designing for this environment means designing within a web of rules that most UX patterns don’t account for.

Platform complexity diagram

A diagram of the platform complexity — how many systems touch a single enrollment or purchase. Exigo, ambassador layers, compliance, autoship. Show what you were designing against.

Opportunity

Most direct sales companies treat their digital tools as administrative necessities. Plexus had the chance to make those tools a genuine competitive advantage — giving ambassadors better data, a better site-building experience, and a back office they actually wanted to use. That’s what drives retention in direct sales.

An ambassador site builder that empowered thousands of non-technical users.

Every Plexus ambassador needed a personal website. I designed a replicated site system with controlled personalization — templates with real customization, brand guardrails built in, visual preview, and SEO optimization. Non-technical users could build a professional web presence without touching code or calling support.

Feature 03 image

The site builder interface — show the template selection, the customization options, and a finished ambassador site. Walk through the system.

A ZenDesk integration suggestion that cut the customer service team by 60%.

I noticed that the most common support contacts were answerable through better self-service design. I proposed a custom-themed ZenDesk integration with structured knowledge base architecture. The company implemented it — and reduced their customer service headcount by 60%. Design thinking applied to operations isn’t overhead. It’s leverage.

Feature 04 image

The ZenDesk help center — the custom theme, the knowledge base structure, the search interface. Show it as a product.

Challenges & Obstacles

Exigo’s API limitations shaped what was technically possible — and not always in design-friendly ways. Compensation plan logic added constraints that surfaced mid-build. And pioneering a design function means every process you create is being invented without a precedent to follow. The work practices I established are still in use at Plexus today because there was no other choice but to build them right the first time.

Supporting image

The back-office dashboard — commissions view, team genealogy, order management. The most complex screen shows the depth of the design work.

Conclusion

Pioneering a design function means building infrastructure — not just interfaces. The processes outlast any single project. The design system multiplies every future effort. The cross-team language you create becomes the way the company works. That’s the difference between a designer and a design practice.
