Card Ritual

A tarot journaling web app designed for practitioners who have outgrown every passive app they've tried. Built solo in three months — concept, design, development, and brand. No outside interpretations. No algorithms. Just you, the cards, and your own lived experience.

Years

2026

Role

Founder, Designer & Developer

Scope

Product Design · AI Integration · SaaS · Brand Strategy · Content

Challenge

Most tarot apps treat the cards like a horoscope. They hand you a predefined meaning and call it a reading. But a real practice doesn’t work that way. Life is too personal and too unpredictable to be interpreted by someone else’s definition. After nine years of daily practice I knew what was missing. An app that trusted the user to develop their own relationship with the cards through lived experience, not handed down interpretations.

Solution

A journaling-first web app built around a single daily loop: pull a card, sit with it, live your day, then come back and write down what you actually experienced. Over time users build their own personal interpretation of every card, not from a book, not from an algorithm, but from their own life. The design keeps the experience intentional and distraction free, because the practice only works if the user does the thinking.

Nine years of personal practice as the only user research that mattered.

Starting from my own daily ritual meant I knew exactly where every existing tool failed. The journaling loop was designed from lived experience, not assumption. That’s a different kind of product clarity than any focus group delivers.

Designed to get out of the way.

The interface leads with one question: what might this card mean for your day? The traditional card interpretation exists but sits below the fold intentionally, available for those who want it, invisible for those who don’t. The user decides how much outside context enters their practice.

For those who want to go further, Go Deeper offers three optional reflection prompts to help unlock more clarity. And for users who want a professional perspective, Mentor Feedback lets them purchase tokens to receive optional feedback from a real tarot reader on their personal reflection.

Every feature is opt-in. The core experience stays clean and uninterrupted for the user who just wants to pull a card and think.

Industry Challenge

The tarot app market is saturated with passive, single-session tools. No product has successfully bridged serious spiritual practice with long-term journaling. Most apps are built by developers who don’t practice, and it shows.

Opportunity

Card Ritual targets the practitioner who has outgrown every app they’ve tried, but also the curious skeptic who has never tried one. Someone intellectually drawn to self-reflection but turned off by mysticism and fortune-telling. By designing around continuity and personal interpretation, returning to past entries, watching your own meaning evolve over time, the product speaks to users who want a reflection practice that feels intelligent, not superstitious. No competitor is speaking to that person at all.

An App Store rejection became a better product decision.

Hitting Apple’s wall during launch forced a web-first pivot. Faster iteration, no gatekeeping, immediate cross-device access. What looked like a setback turned out to be a cleaner path to ship, and the product is stronger for it.

A content strategy built around a real practice and a real person.

A mix of daily reflections using the app, single card pulls with a real deck, and honest takes on tarot superstitions and common misconceptions. No performance, no mysticism theater. Just someone who actually practices sharing what that looks like. Non-follower reach outperformed benchmarks from week one.

Challenges & Obstacles

Building alone is brutal honesty. App Store rejection during launch. Trial users were signing up but not coming back, which meant rebuilding the entire email sequence from scratch. No team to pressure-test decisions, just the product and whether it actually works. That accountability made every design choice sharper.

Conclusion

Card Ritual is the clearest expression of everything I believe about design. Your best work comes from a problem you actually live inside. The app is live, the content is running, and the practice that inspired it is nine years deep.
