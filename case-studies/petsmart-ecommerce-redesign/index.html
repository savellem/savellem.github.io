PetSmart

As Senior Web Designer, I led UX improvements across PetSmart.com — a million-plus-page e-commerce platform — using A/B testing, heat mapping, and user behavior analysis. I redesigned the homepage, global navigation, and product detail pages, launched the Puppy Experience, and managed the Universal Studios Secret Life of Pets partnership, all while coordinating a complete platform migration.

Years

2015

Role

Senior Web Designer

Scope

UX/UI Design · E-Commerce · A/B Testing · Brand Partnerships · Platform Migration

Challenge

Redesigning critical pages on a million-plus-page platform where any change multiplies across massive traffic. The homepage — the most visited page on the entire site — needed to serve dog owners, cat owners, bird owners, fish owners, and reptile people simultaneously. And the whole platform was migrating to a new tech stack mid-project.

Solution

Data-driven design at scale. Every decision validated through A/B tests, heat maps, and behavior analysis before rollout. Close coordination with the in-house dev team throughout the migration. Regular executive presentations to keep design decisions resourced and moving.

Redesigning the highest-traffic page on a million-page site.

The PetSmart homepage wasn’t just a design problem — it was a responsibility. Highest traffic. Most visible. Every pixel change measured. I redesigned the architecture and layout using heat map engagement data, tested variations against live traffic, and maintained the page with continuous improvement after launch.

Transforming global navigation for a catalog that never stops growing.

Traditional mega-menus failed at PetSmart’s product breadth. I restructured the navigation hierarchy based on how users actually moved through the site — not how merchandising wished they would. Simplified category organization, improved mobile nav, and created clear paths to the highest-converting sections.

Industry Challenge

Enterprise e-commerce at this scale is a fundamentally different design discipline. A small navigation change affects millions of page views. A product detail page template covers thousands of SKUs. Design systems aren’t optional at this scale — they’re the only way to maintain quality when the platform is too large for any human to audit manually.

Scale diagram

Visualize what 'one million pages' means. Or a breakdown of page types to show the scope of any single design decision.

Opportunity

PetSmart had the traffic. What it needed was a homepage and navigation that converted that traffic into deeper engagement. With data as the design foundation, every improvement was defensible — to users, to executives, and to the platform migration team.

The Puppy Experience — designing for a life event, not a shopping session.

New puppy owners don’t want a product catalog. They want help. I designed a dedicated hub with age-appropriate product recommendations, educational content, curated bundles, and journey-based navigation — first week, first month, first year. It turned PetSmart into a resource, not just a store.

Managing a high-profile brand partnership with Universal Studios.

The Secret Life of Pets integration required working directly with Universal’s brand team, navigating their IP requirements, coordinating across PetSmart legal, marketing, and merchandising, and delivering on a hard movie-release deadline. Two brand identities. One seamless campaign. No blown timelines.

Feature 04 image

The Secret Life of Pets campaign landing page or promotional materials — show the co-branded execution.

Challenges & Obstacles

A complete platform migration mid-project is the kind of constraint that breaks timelines. Coordinating design requirements with a dev team rebuilding the foundation underneath you requires constant communication, willingness to adapt, and the ability to distinguish what absolutely can’t move from what’s actually flexible.

Conclusion

Enterprise design taught me that scale changes everything. Not just technically — strategically. The decisions that matter most at a million-page platform aren’t the aesthetic ones. They’re the systems ones. The ones that hold up when no one is looking.
